Papua New Guinea 2018

Meeting Results of APEC Papua New Guinea 2018

This year’s conference was attended by more than 50 economic representatives from Australia, Brunei, Canada, Chile, China, Hongkong, Indonesia, Japan, South Korea, Malaysia, New Zealand, Papua New Guinea, The Philippines, Russia, Thailand, USA, Vietnam, and Taiwan. Furthermore, the representative of the APEC Business Advisory Council (ABAC), the Chinese representative of the APEC Policy Partnership for Science, Technology and Innovation (PPSTI), as well as the representative of the Association of Pacific Rim Universities (APRU) were present. The Papua New Guinean minister for Higher Education, Research, Science and Technology Mr. Hon. Pilla Nininga, as well as the minister for Labor and Industrial Relations Mr. Hon. Mehrra Kipefa, were invited to deliver the opening and closing speech.

The conference began with a welcome greeting by Papua New Guinean Labor and Social Protection Network (LSPN) International Co-coordinator Ms. Mary Marola. She welcomed all representatives in Papua New Guinea and wished for a successful conference. She stressed the economic importance of the regional development of human capital and the need for its further progress.

Subsequently, the South Korean chairman of the Human Resource Development Working Group (HRDWG) Professor Park Dong Sun delivered words of greeting. He said that since its establishment in the year 1990, the HRDWG has devoted itself to promote the well-being of the people in the Asia-Pacific Region as well as to realize sustainable and inclusive growth. He explained that since the development of human resources is an important issue in times of digitalization, all member economies have to strengthen their cooperation with the Economic Committee (EC), APRU, PPSTI, and related international organizations. He also sincerely wished for a successful conference.

Lastly, Papua New Guinean Minister for Higher Education, Research, Science and Technology Mr. Hon. Pill Nininga was invited to deliver a few words of greeting. Beside welcoming all representatives of the respective economies, he particularly stressed Papua New Guinea’s efforts to improve the quality of higher education. In order to embrace digitalization, Papua New Guinea started to provide diverse education methods such as E-learning. Furthermore, he expressed his hope to enhance cooperation with the private sectors and the APEC Economies. He also reaffirmed the importance of the priorities set during the 2018 APEC meeting in Papua New Guinea for inclusive growth and sustainable development.

The content of the 35th EDNET conference

The HRDWG consists of three networks, one of them being the Education Network (EDNET). Its main task is to implement educational exchange activities within APEC. The aim of APEC educational activities is the establishment of a strong and vigor learning system within an Economy and to strengthen the function of education in fostering a social, individual, and economic sustainable development. Although there exist differences among the education systems of the respective member economies, during the last years, the Education Network facilitated with great efforts mutual experience sharing and learning.

In order to directly participate in the planning and proposing process of APEC human resource and education related matters, and to share the most exemplary cases of Taiwan’s education with the APEC members, Taiwan was represented by the Department Head of the Department of International and Cross-Strait Education Andi Bi 畢祖安 and Vice Counselor Chen Baoling 陳寶鈴. Ming Chuan University’s Vice President for International Affairs Liu Kuo-Wei 劉國偉, National Taiwan Normal University Professor Chiu Mei-Hung 邱美虹, National Taipei University of Technology Associate Professor Eric Lin 林彥良 and his assistant Miss Xie Bingyan 謝秉諺 did also attend the meeting. They put forward new projects to enhance the cooperation in the education sector between the member economies and shared the progress and results of accomplished activities.

Chair woman Wang Yan 王燕 proposed the plan to establish an APEC-EDNET website. She expressed her expectations for the EDNET website-project to contain the APEC-EDNET projects of all member economies, the latest progress and reforms of the member’s education sectors, educational basic information, basic education, higher education, vocational education, scientific and technological education, and other educational relevant matters At the same time, it should reflect education relevant matters and reports. The first step of the project is the creation of a database that contains important education-related documents and publications of APEC-EDNET. Beside, the information provided by the website shall make it more convenient for member economies to include educational related people and matters that are worth sharing into special column reports. The representative of the USA proposed that the website of the APEC Secretariat was suitable for an immediate update, and that all detailed documents of the project should be uploaded onto this website. If there was an independent APEC-EDNET website, even more detailed documents could be uploaded. Chairwomen Wang Yan also mentioned that the APEC Newsletter: APEC Education Development has already officially been published. If the content of the newsletter was also available on the website, even more educationalists could easier have access to information regarding project contents, the newest education strategies, and programs of action.

Conference Outcomes and Communication

  1. The EDNET International Coordinator Doctor Wang Yan will host an official evening banquet on February 28th. All member economies will sent representatives to attend. Taiwan will be represented by Department Head (Department of International and Cross-Strait Education) Andi Bi and Taiwan Normal University Professor Chiu.
  2. Regarding the “Regional Industry-Academia Collaboration for Talent Development: An Exchange of Skills Training, Internships and Jobs” of Taiwan’s National Taipei University of Technology, there will be an industry-academia collaboration forum in Taipei on 5th to 7th June 2018. During the meeting, the Taiwanese representatives did not only present event-related information,, but also vigorously invited all member economies to participate. After the presentation, South Korea took the initiative to ask about information regarding the forum and expressed great interest to participate.
  3. Regarding the “Human Capacity Building for Globalized Scientific Literacy: Phase I The Best Practice Models for Innovative STEM-Related Education for Teacher Professional Development” proposed by Professor Chiu, a workshop is scheduled for next year in Taipei. Indonesia and Thailand both expressed great interest in coming to Taiwan to participate in that workshop in order to strengthen their teacher professional development. Furthermore, EDNET chairwomen Professor Wang Yan expressed great interest in the implementation results of Professor Chiu’s introduction of new technologies (such as augmented reality) in middle schools, and intended to further inform herself about the project. At the same time, APEC-EDNET Secretariat Program Director Anais Kedgley Laidlaw invited everyone to directly contact him if there were any questions related to the project application procedure. Beside, Professor Chiu engaged in conversations with the representatives of Thailand, USA and Russia to foster possibilities for prospective cooperation.
  4. In June, South Korea’s ministry of education will together with the ministry of education of Hawaii jointly host a global competition of educational app learning in Hawaii and asked Taiwan to recommend two high school students as well as one teacher to participate in this competition. Furthermore, South Korea invited Taiwan to recommend one representative to attend their conference on educational innovation research. This is a self-funded project by South Korea which was granted by APEC last year and starts to be implement this year.
