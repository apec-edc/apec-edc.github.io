APEC STEM Glocalization Workshop

According to the 2015 data proclaimed by the UNESCO Institute for Statistics (UIS), a shortage of talents in science and technology is observed across the Asia-Pacific region. The shortage will very likely restrict economic development and hinder the integration of economies in the region. In response, APEC has been promoting STEM-Plus education in recent years.

In the 2018 EDNET Meeting, Taiwan proposed the ‘Human Capacity Building for Glocalized Scientific Literacy: Phase I The Best Practice Models for Innovative STEM-Related Education for Teacher Professional Development’. Following the proposal in May 2019, we hosted a workshop in Taipei joined by experts and scholars from ten economies.
