MOE Scholarship for People from Asia-Pacific Economic Cooperation Member Economies (MOE APEC Scholarship) is offered by The Ministry of Education, Republic of China (Taiwan), to encourage young international scholars from APEC member economies to undertake research in Taiwan, to provide them with opportunities to increase their understanding of Taiwan’s culture and society, and to promote mutual understanding and interaction between Taiwan and other APEC member economies.

I. Financial Assistance

  • Ph.D. Candidates will receive a monthly research allowance of NT$25,000 and Post Doctoral Fellows will receive a monthly research allowance of NT$40,000.
  • One round-trip, economy-class ticket for the most direct route to Taiwan (The maximum subsidized amount is determined by the MOE.)
  • The terms of fellowship are 2 to 6 months.

II. Eligibility

An international scholar who is a Ph.D. student or a postdoctoral research fellow, with the exception of anyone currently holding an R.O.C. passport, is eligible to apply for an MOE APEC Scholarships. To be more specific, each applicant shall:

  • be a doctoral candidate from an overseas university or a postdoctoral research fellow.
  • be neither a Taiwanese national, nor an overseas Taiwanese compatriot.
  • not already be residing, studying (including internships), teaching or doing research in Taiwan.
  • not have received this award within the last five years.
  • not be a recipient of any other R.O.C. government-sponsored scholarship or subsidy.
  • not be an exchange student while receiving this award.

III. Application Procedure

Applications to undertake a research project in Taiwan in the following year must be submitted to the university, tertiary college, or research organization affiliated with the Ministry of Education—such as the Center for Chinese Studies, or the National Academy for Educational Research—in Taiwan at which the applicant would like to undertake research. All the application material stipulated immediately above must arrive at the host institution no later than September 30 (date of receipt) each year.

Past APEC Scholarship Winners

Sakon Monggoot
  • Year: 2016
  • Origin: PhD candidate, Mae Fah Luang University, Thailand
  • Hosting institute in Taiwan: Graduate Institute of Biotechnology, National Chung Hsing University

Originating from Taiwan’s Chin Shin oolong tea, Ruan Zhi oolong tea No. 17 has been highly popular in Thailand. The objective of this research is to investigate the volatile aromatic and phenolic compounds of Ruan Zhi oolong tea No. 17 cultivated in different altitudes of Chiang Rai, Thailand, by using gas chromatography-mass spectrometry (GC-MS) and liquid chromatography-mass spectrometry (LC-MS) analysis, respectively. Ultimately, the research results can be used to advance the technology of tea production in Thailand and Taiwan.

Geneviève Boivin
  • Year: 2017
  • Origin: Canadian post-doc researcher
  • Hosting institute in Taiwan: Department of Journalism, Chinese Culture University

As the economic, social and diplomatic ties between Canada and Taiwan are strengthening, acculturation studies can be an important foundation to achieve mutual understanding between two cultures and policy design. This study focuses on the role of communication and applies the concept of timing and spatiality to examine how Canadian workers in Taiwan acculturate to a new culture through everyday interactions with their Taiwanese colleagues.

Chutima Tanapichatsakul
  • Year: 2018
  • Origin: PhD student, Mae Fah Luang University, Thailand
  • Hosting institute in Taiwan: Graduate Institute of Biotechnology, National Chung Hsing University

Assam tea is rich in antioxidant activity due to a greater polyphenolic content, such as flavonols. The study aims to investigate teaghrelin-like compounds in different aged and cultivated area in wild forests in northern Thailand, and to compare the amount of teaghrelin-like compounds contained in Chin Shin oolong tea, by using high-performance liquid chromatography (HPLC) and gas chromatography-mass spectrometry (GC-MS).

2019 Scholarship Candidates

Agus Setyo Muntohar (Indonesia)

In earthquake-prone countries such as Indonesia and Taiwan, it is necessary to apply the liquefaction mitigation techniques in liquefaction prone areas. The principle of liquefaction mitigation is increasing the shear strength of sand layers, and using vetiver grass to increase the shear strength is a potential biotechnical method. The study will be tested on the centrifuge model and shaking table, in order to investigate the effect of vetiver root on the change of pore water pressure and ground settlement during the earthquake.

Dimas Bagus Wiranatakusuma (Indonesia)

Given the growing inter-dependence between economies of ASEAN-5 countries, including Indonesia, Malaysia, Philippines, Thailand, Singapore, the study proposes to develop a hypothetical currency unit, called ASEAN Exchange Rates Unit (AERU), for these five countries to achieve the stability of currency and exchange rates in this region under normal and crisis periods. The study then provides concrete steps to be taken to ensure an optimum currency union.

Patcharee Pripdeevech (Thailand)

Recent studies have shown that Pu’er tea is abundant in strictinin, and the content of strictinin in wild trees is significantly higher than that in cultivated shrubs. The objective of this research is to evaluate the antibacterial activities of strictinin as well as its laxative activity of enhancing gastrointestinal motility in an animal model by analyzing samples of Pu’er tea grown in Chiang Mai and Chiang Rai provinces in Thailand.

Sarunpron Khruengsai (Thailand)

This study examines the compounds of oolong tea grown in Thailand, including Taicha No.12, hybrids No. 17, No. 19 and No. 21, especially its teaghrelin-like compounds.

Sodmon Namzhavin (Russia)

Having stayed in Europe for a century and a half, the Kalmyks contributed greatly to strengthening the Russian state. The research aims to create a systematized database that stores archival documents of the XVII-XVIII centuries concerning the history of the Kalmyks. Since a sufficient number of related documents moved after 1949 to Taiwan, it is hoped that these documents can be explored here to further understand the Kalmyks.

Tyler Mark Feezell (USA)

Buxu ci, or “Lyrics for Pacing the Void,” plays an important role in Daoist rituals, and also has a tremendous impact on the Chinese poetic tradition. In Tang dynasty, Buxu hymns became a fixed part in the Daoist rituals. By examining the versions of hymns from Tang, Song, and Ming dynasties, the research aims to understand the nature of Daoist rituals, the use of revealed scripture in Daoist rituals, and the relationship between Daoist music, poetry, meditation, and dance. In addition, as Taiwan is rich in Daoist tradition, as well as experts in the study of Daoism of various periods, this research also aims to clarify the contemporary ritual performance of these hymns and related ritual practices in Taiwan.
