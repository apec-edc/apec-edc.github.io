About APEC

With 21 member economies, Asia-Pacific Economic Cooperation (APEC) is divided into 13 working groups. The participants are mainly representatives sent by each member’s government. In each working group, which can be further divided in several subgroups, members would select a chairman and also a coordinator who is held accountable for coordinating group affairs.

The Department of International and Cross-Strait Education, Ministry of Education is Taiwan’s official contact in the Education Network (EDNET) under the Human Resources Development Working Group (HRDWG). There are two other subgroups in the HRDWG. In the Capacity Building Network (CBN), the Workforce Development Agency, Ministry of Labor is our official contact, and in the Labour and Social Protection Network (LSPN), the Department of General Planning, Ministry of Labor and the Human Resources Development, National Development Council. Also, the Human Resources Development of NDC serves as the chief contact in HRDWG.
